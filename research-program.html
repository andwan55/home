Research Program in Computer Science:
Theory or Applied Code

Due to reaching capacity, we are no longer accepting applicants for our Summer 2023 cohort.

This summer, we are honored to have guest lectures from two Columbia University professors in the CS department!






Course Info

Duration: 17 July 2023 - 10 August 2023, 3.5 week session

Timings: 1:30PM - 2:45PM (PST)

The research program teaches students how to design research questions and implement projects to answer them. Students will work on projects that integrate data science with other fields such as biology, economics, social sciences, political science, or any other field of interest. The program also includes college planning workshops and guest lectures by Columbia professors. By participating in this program, students will create publish-worthy, novel projects.
(recorded classes will be made available for students who have existing time conflicts that are shared with the instructors)

Prerequisites: Basic data science knowledge & python programming knowledge

Instructor(s): Shreya Kochar & Andrew Wang

Columbia guest lecture dates: TBD


Class 1 (Monday, 7/17):
Topic: Introduction to research, discuss potential ideas, talk to potential teammates, etc.
Homework: Read assigned papers for homework, annotate assignments, prepare for discussion for next class

Class 2 (Wednesday, 7/19):
Topic: Finalize teammates based on your interests, class discussion on how to formulate a research question: How do we create novel research?
Homework: Groups assigned papers for homework per their interests, annotate assignments, prepare for discussion

Class 3 (Thursday, 7/20):
Topic: Presentation: We will discuss 1-2 papers and potential research questions from them; Group work: Find papers of interest from our suggestions and other reputable archives and read through them.
Homework: Come up with 3-5 novel questions based off of the papers you have read so far and 3-4 related papers cited in APA per question that could be possibly useful in answering them.

Class 4 (Monday, 7/24):
Topic: Present your research questions and sources/findings so far. Every group will go. 10 minute presentations max, so please keep it short.
Homework: Choose the most fruitful question/interesting question(s) you have from your research, and schedule an advisory meeting with either Shreya or Andrew (scheduling details will be posted later).

Advisory Session:
Discussion: do you need to carry out an experiment? Implement code? Write paper? Visit archives? Access additional resources? Let us know.
Homework: Use the plan of action we create together to start building the first iteration of your project (prospectus, ie an outline).

Class 5 (Wednesday, 7/26):
Topic: Columbia Professor Suman Jana guest lecture (tentative date)
Homework: Work on prospectus. Read the articles and papers assigned for homework.

Class 6 (Thursday, 7/27):
Topic: Prospectus due. Required submission of paper outline (if theory project), rough implementation (if actual code project), etc.
In class: Peer-review (1-2 groups + advisors do critical analysis of your project)
Homework: Refine your sources and your work based on the peer feedback you received in class.
Start working on your first rough draft (which should be your first at creating the project in a fully-functioning capacity).

Class 7 (Monday, 7/31):
Topic: (Presentation) What does research look like in the industry? Group work on rough draft.
Homework: Continue working on your rough draft. Read the articles assigned for homework, as well as the papers.

Class 8 (Wednesday, 8/2):
Topic: Columbia Professor Steven Bellovin guest lecture (tentative date)
Homework: Rough draft submissions due by 11:59 pm. Sign up for an advisory meeting with Shreya or Andrew to review your rough draft.

Advisory Session:
Discussion: How is your project coming along? Our thoughts on your rough draft, additional resources we can offer you, papers/primary sources, etc.
Homework: Modify your final draft to your liking.

Class 9 (Thursday, 8/3):
Topic: (Presentation) How can I get my work published after this course?
Homework: Work on your final project draft. For those working on “implementation” projects, work on adding a small paper alongside your implemented code to explain your results.

Class 10 (Monday, 8/7):
Guest lecture from SuccessKoach: How can I plan best for college?
Homework: Continue working on your final project draft.

Class 11 (Wednesday, 8/9):
Topic: Final day to work on projects in class, meet with advisors and ask for resources/help
Homework: Paper and presentation slide submissions due by 11:59 pm

Class 12 (Thursday, 8/10):
Topic: Project presentations day
